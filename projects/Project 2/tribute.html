A.P.J Abdul Kalam

1931-2015

Missile Man of India

Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam, fondly known as the "Missile Man of India," was a distinguished aerospace scientist and the 11th President of India. Born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu, he rose from humble beginnings to become one of the most revered figures in Indian history. His pivotal role in the nation's civilian space program and military missile development earned him the Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor. Known for his humility, Kalam was a passionate advocate for education, inspiring millions with his vision for a developed India. His legacy continues to ignite the dreams of young minds, making him a true people's president and an eternal symbol of hope and inspiration.
